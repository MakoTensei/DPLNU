 1. A customer experiences a famine and triggers the process to get a Pieper.
2. The Pieper vibrates, indicating that it's time to pick up food.
3. The customer picks up food from the Pieper.
4. The customer eats the food at the court.
5. The court processes the order and triggers the cash payment.
6. The cash payment configures the Pieper.
7. The Pieper triggers the court of first instance.
8. The court of first instance processes the order and triggers the court of justice.
9. The court of justice processes the order and triggers the guest satisfaction and services.
10. The guest satisfaction and services are completed, ending the process.

This flow of events ensures that a customer experiencing a famine is promptly provided with food, leading to their satisfaction and the completion of the order.
