 When a guest enters a restaurant, they are directed to a court of justice where they can select their preferred court. Once they are seated, the restaurant staff will add their order to the system. After the order is placed, the guest is informed that their meal is ready and is served. The guest then picks up their meal, which is a pieper, and the system triggers the preparation of the meal. The staff informs Koch about the ordered meal, and once the meal is finished, the system triggers the pieper. The staff puts the meal in vain and informs the employees about it.
